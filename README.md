# Pokémon Card Sales Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

##  Project Overview

This Power BI dashboard analyzes Pokémon card public sales data across
multiple years, grading companies, and sub-categories.

The objective of this project is to: - Track total sales revenue and
quantity sold - Analyze yearly and monthly sales trends - Compare
grading companies (BGS, CGC, PSA) - Identify high-performing categories
and languages - Enable dynamic filtering for better business insights

------------------------------------------------------------------------

##  Key KPIs

-    Total Sales: **\$130M**
-    Total Quantity Sold: **520K**
-    Years Covered: **2019--2024**
-    Grading Companies: **BGS, CGC, PSA**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

##  Dashboard Features

### 1️ KPI Cards

-   Total Sales
-   Total Quantity Sold

### 2️ Year-wise Sales Analysis

-   Sum of sales by Year
-   Interactive year filter (2019--2024)

### 3️ Monthly Sales Trend

-   Bar chart showing month-wise performance
-   Helps identify seasonal trends

### 4️Grading Company Filter

-   Toggle between BGS, CGC, PSA

### 5️ Detailed Sales Table

-   Year
-   Month
-   Day
-   Total Sales
-   Percentage of Sales
-   Total Quantity

### 6️ Category & Language Analysis

-   Sub-category breakdown
-   Language filter (e.g., Japanese)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Tools & Technologies

-   Power BI Desktop
-   DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
-   Power Query
-   Excel Dataset

------------------------------------------------------------------------

##  Business Insights

-   Sales peaked significantly in 2021.
-   Certain sub-categories such as anniversary and promotional releases
    show higher demand.
-   PSA graded cards contribute a large portion of total revenue.
-   Seasonal spikes observed during early and mid-year months.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

##  How to Use

1.  Open the `.pbix` file in Power BI Desktop.
2.  Use the year slicer to filter specific periods.
3.  Toggle grading companies to compare performance.
4.  Analyze monthly trends and category breakdowns.
